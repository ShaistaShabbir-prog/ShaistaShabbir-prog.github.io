Research Identity

I am a Research Associate at TU Dortmund University (Chair of Virtual Machining) and the Lamarr Institute for Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence. My research develops trustworthy and interpretable AI systems for intelligent manufacturing — with focus on machining process stability, chatter detection, and acoustic monitoring.

My current flagship project is AudioIno — an expert-guided annotation platform and analysis workflow for machining stability labeling, acoustic process monitoring, and data-driven manufacturing intelligence. The broader research agenda aims to answer: How can AI systems reliably support machining decisions with transparent, physically-grounded reasoning?

Long-term research goal: Develop trustworthy and interpretable AI systems for intelligent manufacturing — bridging acoustic signal processing, machine learning, and human-expert knowledge in machining stability analysis.

Current Research Focus

🎙️ AudioIno — Expert-Guided Annotation Platform for Machining Stability
Flagship project · TU Dortmund · 2025–Present

AudioIno is an expert-guided platform for annotation, analysis, and interpretation of machining acoustic signals. It addresses the lack of high-quality labeled datasets for chatter detection and machining stability by combining domain expert knowledge with structured data workflows.

Research questions: How can we reliably label machining stability states from acoustic signals? How can domain expert knowledge be structured and preserved? What annotation quality metrics ensure reproducibility?

Annotation Platform Acoustic Monitoring Chatter Detection Human-in-the-loop Dataset Release (planned)
🔊 Low-Cost Acoustic Monitoring for CNC Milling
Active research · TU Dortmund · 2025–Present

Physics-informed evaluation of low-cost microphone systems (PCB, B5, HI10, TASCAM) for CNC milling stability monitoring. Compares spectrogram similarity, dominant frequency tracking, resonance interpretation, and impact hammer FRF analysis across sensor types to establish cost-effective monitoring baselines.

Potential publication: Physics-Informed Acoustic Monitoring: A Comparative Study of Low-Cost Sensor Systems for Machining Stability

PCB · B5 · HI10 · TASCAM Spectrogram Analysis FRF / Impact Hammer Sensor Comparison
🔧 Tool Wear Prediction — ML Workflow for Condition Monitoring
Research project · 2024–Present

Machine learning workflows for tool-condition monitoring — combining CNN architectures, Random Forest, regression models, and feature engineering pipelines. Research emphasis on explainability: which signal features actually predict wear, and can the model decisions be trusted by engineers?

CNN Random Forest Feature Engineering Explainability
📐 Structural Dynamics & Resonance Analysis (FRF / Impact Hammer)
Supporting research · TU Dortmund · 2025–Present

Frequency Response Function analysis using H1/H2 estimators for resonance identification and coherence analysis. Supports physical interpretation of chatter mechanisms through structural dynamics characterization of the tool-spindle-workpiece system.

FRF Analysis H1/H2 Estimators Resonance Extraction Coherence Analysis
