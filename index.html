About me

I am a Research Associate at TU Dortmund University and the Lamarr Institute for Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence, affiliated with the Chair of Virtual Machining.

My research focuses on Industrial AI for manufacturing — machining stability analysis, chatter detection, acoustic monitoring, explainable machine learning, and trustworthy AI systems.

Currently I work on AudioIno, an expert-guided platform and analysis workflow for machining stability annotation, acoustic process monitoring, and data-driven manufacturing intelligence.

My long-term goal is to develop trustworthy and interpretable AI systems for intelligent manufacturing environments.

AudioIno — Expert Labeling & Analysis Platform for Machining Stability

Active · TU Dortmund 2025–Present

Expert annotation and analysis workflow for machining stability datasets — covering stable, metastable, and chatter regions in acoustic and force-assisted experiments. Supports spectrogram-based annotation, multi-sensor visualization, and NPZ cutout generation for downstream ML workflows.

Low-Cost Acoustic Monitoring for CNC Milling

Active · TU Dortmund 2025–Present

Evaluating whether low-cost microphones (B5, HI10, TASCAM) can capture chatter-relevant acoustic information compared with a PCB reference microphone. Involves spectrogram similarity, dominant-frequency behavior, tooth-pass harmonics, and resonance interpretation via impact hammer testing.
