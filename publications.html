Current Research Manuscripts · TU Dortmund / Lamarr Institute · 2026

AudioIno: Expert-Guided Annotation for Machining Stability and Chatter Analysis

Work in progress Shaista Shabbir · TU Dortmund University / Lamarr Institute · 2026

Presents the AudioIno expert-guided labeling and analysis platform for machining stability annotation, covering stable, metastable, and chatter regions in acoustic and force-assisted machining experiments. Covers annotation methodology, spectrogram-based workflows, and dataset design for Industrial AI.

Low-Cost Acoustic Monitoring for CNC Milling: Spectral Fidelity and Resonance-Guided Interpretation

Work in progress Shaista Shabbir · TU Dortmund University / Lamarr Institute · 2026

Investigates the spectral fidelity of low-cost microphones (B5, HI10, TASCAM) against a PCB reference microphone. Analysis includes spectrogram similarity, dominant-frequency behavior, tooth-pass harmonics, and resonance interpretation via impact hammer testing.

MSCS Research Papers · Virtual University of Pakistan · 2017–2019

Quality Assurance Issues in Software Development (Issues with their Possible Solutions)

Unpublished manuscript Shaista Shabbir · Virtual University of Pakistan · 2018

Examines critical issues in software quality assurance (SQA) faced by organizations and developers — including lack of standardised approaches (CMMI, ISO 9000), absence of dedicated SQA teams, developer-SQA communication gaps, testing/SQA conflation, low customer involvement, and organizational politics. Proposes practical solutions for each identified issue based on literature survey. References NIST data on $59 billion annual US SQA cost.

Software Quality Assurance CMMI IEEE 730 Software Engineering

Communication Problems in Requirements Engineering Process

Unpublished manuscript Shaista Shabbir · Virtual University of Pakistan · 2018

Analyses major communication barriers in requirements engineering (RE) — ineffective communication channels, notation expressiveness limitations, and organizational/cultural factors. Investigates how communication failures cause project overruns and proposes solutions including enriched notations, face-to-face stakeholder contact, and reduction of organizational politics in requirements gathering.
