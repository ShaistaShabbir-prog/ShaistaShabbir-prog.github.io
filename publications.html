Current Research Manuscripts · TU Dortmund / Lamarr Institute

AudioIno: Expert-Guided Annotation for Machining Stability and Chatter Analysis

Work in progress Shaista Shabbir · TU Dortmund University / Lamarr Institute 2026

Expert-guided labeling and analysis platform for machining stability annotation, covering stable, metastable, and chatter regions in acoustic and force-assisted machining experiments. Discusses annotation methodology, spectrogram-based workflows, and dataset design for Industrial AI.

Machining Stability Chatter Detection Human-in-the-loop Industrial AI

Low-Cost Acoustic Monitoring for CNC Milling: Spectral Fidelity and Resonance-Guided Interpretation

Work in progress Shaista Shabbir · TU Dortmund University / Lamarr Institute 2026

Investigates the spectral fidelity of low-cost microphones (B5, HI10, TASCAM) against a PCB reference for capturing chatter-relevant acoustic information in CNC milling. Analysis includes spectrogram similarity, dominant-frequency behavior, tooth-pass harmonics, and resonance interpretation via impact hammer testing.

Acoustic Monitoring Low-Cost Sensing Machining Dynamics Signal Processing

Tool Wear Prediction from Multimodal Machining Signals

Work in progress Student thesis collaboration · TU Dortmund University 2026

Investigates tool condition monitoring using force, temperature, and acoustic machining signals. Addresses shortcut learning, generalization across cutting conditions, and ablation analysis of feature contributions to CNN and traditional ML models.

Tool Wear CNN Feature Engineering Explainable ML
MSc Research Papers · Virtual University of Pakistan

Quality Assurance Issues in Software Development

Term paper · Not published Shaista Shabbir · Virtual University, Lahore 2017–2018

Investigates issues faced by managers, developers, and organisations in software quality assurance during the development lifecycle. Surveys the literature on SQA standards (CMMI, ISO 9000, IEEE 730), identifies nine recurring issues — including lack of standardisation, developer-QA friction, and organisational politics — and proposes concrete solutions for each.

Software Quality SQA CMMI Software Engineering

Submitted as course paper for CS706 at Virtual University of Pakistan.

Communication Problems in Requirement Engineering Process

Term paper · Not published Shaista Shabbir · Virtual University, Lahore 2017–2018

Examines communication barriers in requirements engineering — covering ineffective channels, notation limitations, and organisational/cultural factors. Proposes enriched notations, face-to-face elicitation, and structured stakeholder engagement strategies to reduce requirements defects and project failures.

Requirements Engineering Communication Software Quality NLP

Submitted as course paper for CS708 at Virtual University of Pakistan.
